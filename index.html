why does it matter?

Maintaining a regular exercise routine and eating a balanced diet are crucial for teens because they support healthy growth and development. Physical activity enhances not only your energy levels but also cognitive function and emotional well-being, which can improve academic performance. Consuming a variety of nutrient-rich foods provides essential vitamins and minerals that your body needs to operate efficiently. Establishing these healthy habits during adolescence can reduce the risk of chronic diseases like obesity, diabetes, and heart conditions later in life. Ultimately, prioritizing exercise and nutrition lays the foundation for long-term physical and mental wellness.
